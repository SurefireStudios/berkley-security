Is Your Business Protected While Employees Are on Summer Vacation?

By Berkley Security Inc. | 6 min read

Table of Contents

  • Common Vulnerabilities Businesses Overlook
  • How Modern Security Systems Help Protect Your Business
  • Monitor Your Business From Anywhere
  • Summer Security Checklist

Summer brings long days, warmer weather, and a chance to finally relax. For many companies, it is a period when operations slow down, team members take time off, and business hours might change. While this downtime is great for employees, it can leave commercial properties vulnerable.

Fewer people in the office means fewer eyes on your property. Reduced foot traffic and empty offices can create opportunities for theft, vandalism, and unauthorized access. As a business owner or property manager, the last thing you want is a frantic phone call interrupting your own family trip because your facility was compromised.

Let's look at why summer creates specific security challenges and how investing in professional business security systems ensures your commercial property stays safe while your team takes a well-deserved break.

Why Summer Can Increase Security Risks

When employees start taking PTO, the daily rhythm of a business shifts. Normal routines are broken, and criminals often pay close attention to these changes.

Consider what happens during the summer months:

  • Empty buildings: Many offices run on skeleton crews during July and August, leaving areas of the building unmonitored.
  • Altered routines: Criminals often watch businesses to learn when staff arrive and leave. A sudden drop in activity signals that a location is prime for a break-in.
  • Unattended deliveries: Without a dedicated receptionist or warehouse manager on duty, packages and inventory might sit outside or in unsecured loading docks.
  • Weekend travel: More long weekends mean facilities are left empty from Friday afternoon until Tuesday morning, offering an extended window for theft.

Common Vulnerabilities Businesses Overlook

Even if you have basic security measures in place, summer operations can expose hidden vulnerabilities. Local businesses often miss these common risks until it is too late:

  • Doors left unlocked: Temporary staff covering for vacationing employees may not know the proper closing procedures.
  • Outdated alarm systems: Older alarms that rely on landlines can be easily defeated or may fail to communicate properly.
  • Poor lighting and blind spots: Overgrown summer landscaping can block exterior lighting and create hiding spots near windows and doors.
  • No camera coverage: A lack of commercial security cameras means you have no way to verify who is entering the property.
  • Employees sharing keys: Physical keys are often passed around to cover shifts, making it impossible to track who actually has access to the building.
  • Unsecured inventory: Valuable assets left in plain sight become tempting targets when there are fewer staff members around.

How Modern Security Systems Help Protect Your Business

You cannot be at your business 24 hours a day, but a modern commercial security system can. Today's technology provides a comprehensive shield around your facility, protecting against both internal and external threats.

  • Smart Alarms: Modern business alarm systems use cellular connections that cannot be cut by intruders.
  • HD Cameras & Cloud Video: High-definition cameras with night vision capture clear footage of your property, storing video securely in the cloud so it cannot be tampered with or destroyed on-site.
  • Mobile Notifications: Receive instant text or push alerts if a door is propped open, motion is detected in a restricted area, or an alarm triggers.
  • Access Control: Eliminate physical keys. Use keycards or mobile credentials to grant and revoke access instantly. You will always know who entered the building and exactly when.
  • Smart Locks: Did the last person to leave forget to lock the back door? Smart locks can automatically secure the building on a schedule.
  • Fire Monitoring: A fire can destroy a business in minutes. Connected fire alarm systems immediately alert the fire department, even if the building is completely empty.

Monitor Your Business From Anywhere

You should be able to enjoy your own summer vacation without constantly worrying about your property. Modern commercial security systems allow you to manage your business directly from your smartphone.

Whether you are on a beach in Florida or visiting family across the country, a mobile app gives you complete control. You can view live camera feeds to check on the warehouse, review activity logs to ensure the morning crew arrived on time, and even lock or unlock doors remotely for contractors or delivery drivers.

Summer Security Checklist

Before the peak vacation season hits, take a walk through your facility and complete this quick security audit:

  • ✓ Test alarm system: Ensure all door contacts, window sensors, and motion detectors are communicating properly.
  • ✓ Verify camera coverage: Check camera lenses for dirt or spider webs, and ensure no landscaping is blocking the view.
  • ✓ Update employee access: Deactivate codes or keycards for former employees and set up temporary access for summer hires.
  • ✓ Review emergency contacts: Make sure your monitoring company has the correct, updated phone numbers for keyholders who are actually in town.
  • ✓ Trim landscaping: Keep bushes trimmed below windows and tree branches away from the roof to maintain clear sightlines.
  • ✓ Check exterior lighting: Replace burnt-out bulbs and test motion-activated floodlights.
  • ✓ Test backup power: Summer thunderstorms in the Gulf South often cause power outages. Ensure your system's battery backup is functioning.
  • ✓ Secure valuable inventory: Move high-value items away from windows and into locked storage areas.

Why Professional Monitoring Matters

Having cameras and alarms is a great start, but commercial alarm monitoring is what truly protects your business when no one is around.

If a break-in occurs at 2:00 AM while you are asleep in a different time zone, a self-monitored system relies entirely on you waking up to a phone notification. 24/7 alarm monitoring removes that risk. Professional operators receive the alert instantly, verify the emergency, and dispatch local police or fire departments immediately.

This rapid response drastically reduces downtime, minimizes property damage, and ensures that emergencies are handled swiftly, without relying on neighbors or passersby to report suspicious activity.

Do not wait until a break-in occurs to evaluate your property's vulnerabilities. Taking proactive steps now ensures that your facility, inventory, and employees remain safe throughout the summer months.
