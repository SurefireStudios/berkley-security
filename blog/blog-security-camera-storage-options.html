Security Camera Storage: Local NVR vs Cloud vs Hybrid

Updated April 2026 | By Berkley Security Inc. | 7 min read

Quick Answer:

3 storage options: Local NVR ($300-$800 one-time, no monthly fees, 30+ days). Cloud ($3-$30/month per camera, remote access, off-site backup). Hybrid (local NVR + cloud backup of key clips, best of both). Most homes need a local NVR. Businesses needing remote access benefit from hybrid. See our video surveillance page.

Your security cameras are only useful if the footage is accessible, protected, and stored long enough to be useful. Nothing is worse than discovering a break-in and finding your recording was overwritten or the storage device was stolen. Here is how to choose the right storage for your cameras.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best camera storage?

Most homes: local NVR (no monthly fees). Businesses: hybrid (local + cloud backup). Renters: cloud-only.

How long should I keep footage?

Residential: 14-30 days. Small business: 30-60 days. Retail: 30-90 days. Regulated: 90+ days.

How much storage do cameras need?

One 4MP camera: ~1 TB/month (24/7) or ~300 GB/month (motion). 4 cameras with motion: 1-2 TB/month. 8 TB NVR covers 4-8 months.

What is an NVR?

Network Video Recorder. A dedicated device recording IP camera footage to built-in hard drives. No internet or monthly fees needed for basic use.

Is cloud storage worth it?

Yes for remote access, off-site backup (fire/theft safe), and easy clip sharing. The trade-off is ongoing monthly cost and internet dependency.

What if my NVR is stolen?

All evidence is gone. Solutions: lock the NVR in a closet/safe, use cloud backup for key cameras (hybrid), or enable RAID mirroring.
