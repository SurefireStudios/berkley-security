Retail Loss Prevention: The 5-Layer Strategy to Reduce Shrinkage

Updated April 2026 | By Berkley Security Inc. | 8 min read

Quick Answer:

US retail shrinkage hit $112 billion in 2023. Effective loss prevention uses 5 layers: visible surveillance cameras (50-70% shoplifting reduction), EAS anti-theft tags, POS integration to catch internal theft, staff training, and after-hours monitoring. Systems start at $2,000-$5,000 for small stores with ROI in 3-6 months.

If you run a retail business in Mississippi, shrinkage is eating your profits. The average retail store loses 1.4% of revenue to theft, errors, and fraud. For a store doing $500,000 annually, that is $7,000 in losses. A properly designed loss prevention system pays for itself within months.

The 5-Layer Loss Prevention Strategy

1

Surveillance Camera System

Visible cameras at entrances, all registers (two angles), high-value merchandise, stockroom, and receiving dock. Visible cameras reduce shoplifting 50-70%. 4MP+ for facial ID. 30+ days recording. See our small business camera guide.

2

EAS Anti-Theft Tags

Electronic tags on portable high-value items (clothing, electronics, cosmetics). Tags trigger alarms at store exits if not deactivated at the register. Hard tags, soft labels, and spider wraps available. Self-funded within months from reduced shrinkage.

3

POS Integration

Camera recordings synced with register transactions. When a void, refund, or suspicious transaction occurs, you can pull up the exact camera footage. This catches sweet-hearting (not ringing items for friends), void fraud, and cash drawer discrepancies. Critical for the 28% of losses from employee theft.

4

Employee Training

Train staff on greeting every customer (deterrent), recognizing concealment behavior, proper register procedures, and how to report suspicious activity safely. A trained team is your most cost-effective loss prevention tool.

5

After-Hours Monitoring

Intrusion alarm with door/window sensors, motion detectors, and 24/7 monitoring for police dispatch during break-in attempts. Most retail burglaries occur after hours through roof access, rear doors, or smash-and-grab entries.

Frequently Asked Questions

Best way to prevent retail theft?

5 layers: visible cameras, EAS tags, POS integration, trained staff, and after-hours monitoring. No single solution works alone.

How much does retail security cost?

Small store: $2K-$5K equipment, $40-$75/month. Mid-size: $5K-$15K. ROI typically 3-6 months from reduced shrinkage.

Where should I put cameras?

Every entrance, all registers (two angles: customer face and cash drawer), high-value areas, stockroom, receiving dock, and employee areas.

What % of theft is employee theft?

About 28-30% of shrinkage. Internal theft averages larger losses per incident than shoplifting, making POS cameras essential.

Do cameras reduce shoplifting?

Visible cameras reduce shoplifting 50-70%. Key: visible placement + warning signage + active monitoring by staff.

What is EAS?

Electronic Article Surveillance: tags on merchandise that alarm at exits if not deactivated at the register. Self-funded from reduced shoplifting.
