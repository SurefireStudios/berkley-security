How to Choose the Right Security Company in Mississippi

Updated April 2026 | By Berkley Security Inc. | 8 min read

Quick Answer:

The right security company should be licensed in Mississippi, conduct an on-site assessment before quoting, use background-checked technicians, offer no long-term contracts, and provide local service (not scheduling from a distant city). Avoid door-to-door salespeople offering "free equipment" tied to 5-year contracts. Use our 8-point checklist below to evaluate any company before signing.

Choosing a security company is a decision that directly affects your family's safety, your insurance costs, and your monthly budget for years to come. With dozens of companies advertising across Mississippi, from local installers to national chains to door-to-door salespeople, knowing how to separate the qualified professionals from the fly-by-night operators is critical.

The 8-Point Evaluation Checklist

Use this checklist when evaluating any security company. A reputable company will satisfy all eight points without hesitation:

1

Mississippi License Verification

Mississippi requires alarm companies to hold a valid state contractor's license. Ask for the number and verify it through the Board of Contractors at (601) 354-6161. No license = immediate disqualification.

2

Background-Checked Technicians

Technicians will know your alarm codes, camera positions, and entry vulnerabilities. Insist that every person entering your home has passed a thorough background check. This is non-negotiable.

3

On-Site Assessment Before Quoting

A proper system design requires visiting your property to assess entry points, camera angles, sensor placement, and signal strength. Companies quoting over the phone are selling generic kits.

4

Contract Terms (or No Contract)

Review contract length, auto-renewal clauses, early termination fees, and rate increase provisions. The best companies do not require long-term contracts.

5

Equipment Ownership

Ask: "Do I own the equipment?" If you are leasing, the equipment goes back when the contract ends. You want to own your equipment outright.

6

Local Service Response Time

Ask how quickly they can respond to a service call. Local companies offer same-day or next-day. National chains may take 1-2 weeks from a distant dispatch center.

7

Warranty Coverage

What is covered? For how long? Does it include parts and labor? A strong warranty backed by a local team is worth more than a vague national warranty you cannot enforce.

8

Years in Business and Reviews

Check Google Reviews, BBB rating, and how long they have been in business. Long-standing companies with real local reviews have earned their reputation.

Red Flags: When to Walk Away

  • Door-to-door salespeople offering "free equipment" with same-day signing pressure
  • Cannot provide a license number or becomes evasive when asked
  • Quotes a price over the phone without visiting your property
  • Requires contracts longer than 36 months with high early termination fees
  • "Limited time offer" pressure designed to prevent you from comparing competitors
  • No local office or technicians in your area
  • Cannot explain what happens to equipment if you cancel

Questions to Ask Before Signing

Print this list and ask every company you evaluate:

  1. What is your Mississippi license number?
  2. Are all technicians background-checked?
  3. Will you assess my property before quoting?
  4. Do I own the equipment, or am I leasing?
  5. What is the contract length? Can I go month-to-month?
  6. What is the early termination fee?
  7. How fast can you respond to a service call?
  8. What does the warranty cover, and for how long?

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I verify a company's MS license?

Ask for the license number and verify through the Mississippi Board of Contractors at (601) 354-6161. No license = do not hire.

Local or national: which is better?

Local companies offer same-day service, no contracts, lower rates, and technicians in your area. National chains charge more and schedule from distant offices.

What contract terms should I watch for?

Watch for auto-renewal clauses, early termination fees ($500-$1,500), equipment leasing vs ownership, and rate increase provisions during the contract.

Should they visit my home before quoting?

Yes, always. Proper system design requires assessing entry points, camera angles, sensor placement, and signal strength. Phone quotes mean generic kits.

What questions should I ask?

License number, background checks, on-site assessment, equipment ownership, contract length, termination fee, service response time, and warranty details.

What are the biggest red flags?

Door-to-door with free equipment, no license number, phone-only quotes, contracts over 36 months, same-day signing pressure, and no local office.
