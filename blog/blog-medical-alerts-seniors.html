Medical Alert Systems for Seniors: Complete Comparison Guide

Updated April 2026 | By Berkley Security Inc. | 8 min read

Quick Answer:

Medical alert systems connect seniors to emergency help in seconds via a wearable button. 3 types: in-home pendants ($25-$35/month), mobile GPS units ($35-$45/month), and integrated systems on a home security panel ($30-$50/month total). Look for automatic fall detection, waterproof design, and 24/7 monitoring. See our medical alert systems page.

Every 11 seconds, an older adult visits an emergency room for a fall. Every 19 minutes, an older adult dies from a fall. Medical alert systems provide instant access to help when a senior cannot reach a phone, often the difference between a treatable injury and a life-threatening situation.

How Medical Alerts Work

  1. Senior presses the button on their pendant/wristband (or automatic fall detection triggers)
  2. The device connects to the 24/7 monitoring center via base station or cellular
  3. A trained operator speaks with the senior through the two-way speaker
  4. Based on the situation, the operator dispatches EMS, contacts family, or sends non-emergency help
  5. The monitoring center stays on the line until help arrives

Why Fall Detection Matters

Many falls result in the person being unable to reach a phone or press a button. They may lose consciousness, become disoriented, or be physically unable to reach the pendant. Automatic fall detection uses accelerometers to detect a fall impact and contacts the monitoring center without the senior pressing anything. This feature saves lives when seconds count.

What to Look For

  • Waterproof pendant: Must be worn in the shower (the #1 fall location)
  • Two-way communication: Speak and hear through the pendant or base station
  • Automatic fall detection: Critical for seniors at high fall risk
  • Battery life: Minimum 24-hour battery on mobile units, rechargeable
  • No long-term contract: Month-to-month availability
  • UL-listed monitoring center: Same quality as security alarm monitoring

Frequently Asked Questions

How do medical alerts work?

Press the button on the pendant. The monitoring center connects, assesses the situation, and dispatches EMS or contacts family.

How much do they cost?

In-home: $25-$35/month. Mobile GPS: $35-$45/month. Integrated with security: $30-$50/month total for both.

What is fall detection?

The pendant detects a fall impact and automatically calls the monitoring center without the senior pressing a button. Critical when they cannot press it.

Do pendants work in the shower?

Yes. Quality pendants are waterproof (IP67). Bathrooms are the #1 fall location. The pendant should be worn 24/7 including in the shower.

Does Medicare cover medical alerts?

Original Medicare does not. Some Medicare Advantage plans do. Mississippi Medicaid may cover for qualifying individuals. Check your specific plan.

Which type is best?

Homebodies: in-home pendant. Active seniors: mobile GPS. Those who also need security: integrated panel system (one monthly payment for both).
