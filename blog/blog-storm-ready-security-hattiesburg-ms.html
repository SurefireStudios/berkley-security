Hattiesburg Storm Preparedness Guide

Storm-Ready Security Systems for Hattiesburg, MS

Updated April 2026 | By Berkley Security Inc. | 7 min read

Quick Answer:

Yes, professional security systems work during power outages. Quality systems include battery backup (24-72 hours) and cellular monitoring that operates independently of your internet and power. When a Mississippi storm knocks out electricity and phone lines, your Hattiesburg home stays monitored and protected. Berkley Security installs storm-hardened systems with surge protection standard on every Pine Belt installation.

Hattiesburg and the Pine Belt know storms. From the devastating tornadoes that have struck the Hub City to hurricane remnants that push inland with 70+ mph winds, severe weather is a reality of life in southeast Mississippi. When those storms hit, they expose a critical vulnerability: homes are most at risk for break-in and damage precisely when power is out, residents are evacuated, and traditional communication is down.

This guide explains how a properly designed security system protects your Hattiesburg home before, during, and after Mississippi storms.

Before the Storm: Preparation Features

  • Weather alerts delivered to your security app notify you of tornado warnings, severe thunderstorm warnings, and hurricane watches for Forrest County
  • Remote system check lets you verify your alarm is armed, battery is charged, and all sensors are operational before the storm arrives
  • Automated storm scene arms your system, locks all doors, closes the garage, and enables all cameras to continuous recording with a single tap
  • Water leak sensors placed in vulnerable areas alert you to roof leaks or flooding as the storm progresses

During the Storm: How Your System Stays Active

What Keeps Working

  • + Alarm panel (battery backup)
  • + Door and window sensors
  • + Motion detectors
  • + Cellular monitoring communication
  • + Smart lock codes and functions
  • + Smoke and CO detectors
  • + Medical alert pendants

Needs UPS Backup

  • ~ Security cameras (need UPS)
  • ~ NVR recording (need UPS)
  • ~ Wi-Fi router (need UPS)
  • ~ Smart thermostat display
  • ~ Smart home hub

UPS systems extend camera recording 2-8 hours during outages.

After the Storm: Post-Storm Protection

Here is what you need to know: The period after a storm is when your home is most vulnerable. Power is out across the neighborhood, streets are blocked with debris, and police are overwhelmed with emergency calls. This is when break-ins and looting spike. Your security system provides critical protection during this window:

  • Compromised entry detection. If a storm breaks a window or displaces a door, your sensors detect intrusion through those openings and trigger alerts.
  • Evacuation protection. If you evacuate to a shelter or family member's home, your system monitors your empty Hattiesburg property and dispatches police for any unauthorized entry.
  • Remote damage assessment. Check security cameras from your phone to assess storm damage before driving back to the property.
  • Fire detection. Post-storm fires from damaged electrical wiring are a real risk. Monitored smoke detectors catch these fires early.

Surge Protection: Protecting Your Investment

Mississippi lightning destroys thousands of electronic devices every year. A single surge can destroy your alarm panel, cameras, NVR, and smart devices in one strike. We install commercial-grade surge protection on every Hattiesburg installation:

  • Surge protectors on all power lines feeding security equipment
  • Data line surge protection on camera cables and network connections
  • Properly grounded installation per NEC standards
  • UPS battery systems for cameras and NVR on premium installations

Frequently Asked Questions: Storm Security in Hattiesburg

Does security work during power outages?

Yes. Battery backup powers the panel for 24-72 hours. Cellular monitoring works independently of internet and power. Your home stays protected during extended outages.

How does cellular monitoring help in storms?

Cellular towers have backup power and redundancy. When your power, internet, and phone fail, your alarm communicates via cell network. It is the most storm-resilient monitoring method.

Can security protect my home after storm damage?

Yes. Sensors detect entry through broken windows and displaced doors. Your system monitors for intrusion during the vulnerable post-storm period when neighborhood power is out and looting risk peaks.

Do cameras record during outages?

With UPS battery backup, cameras and NVRs continue recording for 2-8 hours during outages. Without UPS, cameras stop when power fails but the alarm system continues on its own battery.

Do you install surge protection?

Yes. Commercial-grade surge protection on power and data lines is standard on every Pine Belt installation. This prevents lightning from destroying your security equipment.

What if a storm damages my security system?

Contact us immediately. We prioritize storm-damage service calls. Storm damage is typically covered by homeowner's insurance, and we provide documentation for claims.
