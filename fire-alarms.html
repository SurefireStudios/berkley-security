Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about fire alarm systems in Mississippi and Louisiana.

What type of fire alarm system do I need for my business?
Commercial properties in Mississippi require NFPA 72 compliant fire alarm systems. Requirements depend on building size, occupancy type, and local fire marshal codes. Berkley Security designs code-compliant systems including smoke detectors, heat sensors, pull stations, and horn/strobe notification devices with 24/7 central station monitoring.
How often do fire alarms need to be inspected?
Commercial fire alarm systems in Mississippi require annual inspections per NFPA 72 and local fire marshal requirements. Some components like smoke detectors require semi-annual testing. Berkley Security provides scheduled inspection services to keep your system compliant and functioning properly.
Do residential homes need monitored fire alarms?
While not legally required for most residential homes in Mississippi, monitored fire alarms provide 24/7 fire department dispatch even when you are away or asleep. Monitored smoke and heat detectors can be integrated into your existing security system at minimal additional cost.
What is the difference between smoke detectors and heat detectors?
Smoke detectors respond to particles of combustion and are ideal for bedrooms, hallways, and living areas. Heat detectors respond to temperature changes and are better for kitchens, garages, and attics where normal activities may produce smoke. A properly designed fire system uses both types in appropriate locations.
Can fire alarms be integrated with my security system?
Yes, Berkley Security integrates fire detection with your burglar alarm on a single monitoring platform. Smoke detectors, heat sensors, and CO detectors all communicate through the same panel and are monitored 24/7 by the same central station that handles your security system.
